THEY TOO HAVE LIVES
By Anshita Gera

Handworking colourful lives turning into grey ones,
Half smiles transforming into millions of tear crumbs!
Their soul wanting to have life's sparkle instead of dust,
They only got a magician who threw them out of disgust.

Walking in the earth's never ending platform,
Carrying immense loads, it seems like a heavy thunderstorm!
Even if they have air for satisfaction of lungs,
How can they fill their empty tums?
Barely any food and money, but gazillion struggles.
Such low hopes, there came many covid-19 bubbles.

Imagine- people trying to cover miles without any question.
Then resting in the railway track due to extreme exhaustion,
Having deep sleep, no ears to listen to the approaching sound,
Those fast rotating wheels crushing their bodies in metallic ground.
Oh, i wish i was there with limitless force, to stop Aurangabad's train,
And bring glitter to people's lives and relieve their pain.

Even collision has a massive impact,
Wherein two hefty bodies get individually slapped.
Oh, i wish i was there with limitless force, to stop those trucks from crashing.
Many migrant workers were sleeping, and in another moment, vanishing.
The situation in UP's Auraiya was disheartening.
And for me, infinitely frightening.

To all the migrant workers, I am with you,
And hopefully the world does not pass through.
You have my heart always and forever,
Indeed, your suffering was never blur!
